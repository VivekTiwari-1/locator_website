Hello There

We Are Young But Bold

Rotaract Club of Sindri is the youth wing of Rotary International aimed at organizing various socio-cultural events and developing managerial skills. Our main purpose is to provide an opportunity for young men and women to enhance the knowledge and skills that will assist them in personal development, to address the physical and social needs of their communities, and to promote better relations between all people worldwide through a framework of friendship and service.

Why to join us

There are plenty of reasons to join us

The Opportunity to Serve

Rotaract is a social service club. Its business is mankind. Its product is service. This is perhaps the best reason for becoming a Rotaractor: the chance to do something for the people.

It’s International

When you join Rotaract, you’re not just joining a local volunteer group, but an international community. There are 9522 Rotaract clubs and 291,006 members in 187 countries around the world .

Leadership Development

Rotary is an organisation of leaders and successful people. Serving in Rotary positions is like a college education. Leadership is all about learning how to motivate, infuence and lead leaders.

It looks great on your resume

Rotaract is afliated with Rotary – one of the world’s top service clubs – and having it on your resume sets you apart. With 1.2 million Rotarians around the world, chances are that person interviewing you is one of them.

Public Speaking Skills

Many individuals who joined Rotaract were uncomfortable about speaking in public. Rotaract develops confidence and skill in public communication and the opportunity to practice and perfect these skills.

Prestige

Rotaract members are prominent people: leaders of quality, the professions, art, sports, and all disciplines. Rotary is the oldest and most prestigious service club in the world.

Friendship Fellowship

Rotaract provides one of the most basic platform: the need of friendship and fellowship. It is one of the two reasons why Rotary began in 1905.

Entertainment

Rotaract holds conferences, conventions, assemblies, and institutes that provide entertainment in addition to information, education, and service.

Nice People

Rotaractors are the nicest people on the face of the earth.

Fun!

Rotaract is a fun and exciting place to be.

Eye Relief Camp

The Rotaract Club of Sindri successfully organised its 4-days 54th Eye Relief Camp at Public Health Centre, Gaushala, Sindri. The event was inaugurated by Kishore Kaushal, SSP Dhanbad on 25th Feb, 2019. Mr. Arvind Kumar, ED of SAIL, Chasnala Colliery Division was also present on this occasion along with other notable dignitaries. Registration for the cataract operations began on 26th February, 2019 whereby patients were screened by a panel of doctors according to their medical conditions. A total of 63 cataract operations were performed with 38 female and 25 male patients. The operations were carried out totally free of cost under the aegis of Rotary Club of Sindri. The patients were released after a thorough medical examination a day after their operations on 28th February 2019. Members of Rotaract Club of Sindri worked actively for the success of this noble cause.
Regards
Rotaract Club Of Sindri

Sandeshe Aate Hai

Web Design

Amongst the continuous report of tensions on the borders, Rotaract Club of Sindri came up with a unique idea to celebrate the efforts that soldiers put in to give us a safe and secure life. They don't hesitate to risk their lives for our safety and prosperity. When it comes to thinking about their sacrifices and of their families, there is indeed a great emotional upheaval within each of us. Rotaract thereby introduced an event, 'संदेशे आते हैं', that gave students a way to pen down their feelings and gratitude, in the form of letters addressed to soldiers and martyrs. It commenced on 8th of July and was called off on the 10th of August with about 40 entries. The best letter was declared as winner on the 15th of August. It gave an immense amount of acknowledgement to what Indian youth feel about their country and especially of people shielding it day and night

SharadUtsav

Web Design

The Rotaract club of Sindri did not fail to leave its mark on BIT Sindri's first SHARADUTSAV. With three events under our charge, we Rotaractors truly made sure that the BITians had a blast. Starting with the first night ,is, the 15th of November , we hosted our music gala ENCORE ; a Bollywood Rock and pop singing competition open to all but with a suprise element, the Band . This had everyone jumping on their feet with their hands up in the air. On the second day , 16th of November, we organised two conjunctures - an exhibition SANDARSHAN and CHAKRAVYUH, the Treasure Hunt. Sandarshan showcased a glimpse of Rotaract's major activities through the years like the Eye Relief Camp, Blood Donation Camp, Joy of Giving drive, visit to Jeevan Jyoti School and so on, on the Cartoon Network theme. Charkravyuh was a treasure hunt organised exclusively for the first year students who where seen excitedly rummaging through the college campus looking for clues. The top four teams then had to participate in a Debate and finally a Roadies based round from which the winner was selected. All in all, the Rotaract club's journey at the Sharad utsav was truly a memorable one as the club proved its mettle in organising a cultural fest.

ERC at Rikhiyapeeth

"Keeping in mind the yearly participation of Rotaract Club of Sindri in the Eye Camp at Rikhiyapeeth, a team of 20 members arrived on 3rd September 2019. The team was very warmly welcomed. The ashram environment was very soothing and delightful. Our work began from the registration desk to the doctor’s cabin where the patients were checked for cataract, then to the fitness checking room where the nurses tested blood pressure, blood sugar and pulse rate. Followed by the surgery processing room where it was confirmed that the patients were fit for surgery and necessary data were taken care of. Heading towards the photography room where the batch for the operation was allotted and the patient's photograph was taken. All the necessary data like name, OPD no., batch of operation etc. were recorded. All the way our main work was to ensure the correct on and off paper data entry. We marked our departure on 7th September 2019. Stay at Rikhiyapeeth was a perfect combination of peaceful Ashram Life along with responsibility and service.
Regards
Rotaract Club Of Sindri

Blood Donation Camp

“A single pint can save three lives, a single gesture can create a million smiles". Rotaract Club of BIT Sindri organised a 'Blood Donation Camp' in the college premises to collect blood for the needy. In association with NSS and Jalan Hospital, the club ventured to bring into the students, importance of blood donation. Prof. S.C Dutta inaugurated the event and congratulated the club members for their overwhelming involvement. With the exuberant participation of the students, the quota of blood counted to 35 units. Donors were provided with the refreshments and were encouraged for further blood donations. NSS in-charge, Prof. Ranjit along with Prof. Raghunandan marked their sincere presence in the campaign. Our director, Dr D.K. Singh praised Rotaractors for their efforts in successful conduction of the camp. Rotaract assured of similar camps further to extend its hand in the benefit of underprivileged.
Regards
Rotaract Club Of Sindri

Walk of Life Rally

We, the members of Rotaract Club of Sindri, took some moments out of our monotonous college schedule for the special children of Jeevan Jyoti School. We volunteered in the rally organised in the World Disability Day, guiding the serene souls who lacked spark in their eyes. Walking hand in hand with them instigated the epiphany that they feel the world, making their aura as pure as possible for a human, while we visually observe the world and judge everything based on our convenience. They may be visually impaired, but they are more human and livelier than we ever could be. The Rally commenced from Jeevan Jyoti School and concluded with a lively picnic at the Dhanbad Club.
Regards
Rotaract Club Of Sindri

Volunteering at Tundi

A swift travel through the nature and we were in Tundi. The camp was organised at a government school Saraswati Swarup Vidya Mandir by the Rotary Club of Dhanbad. We met the enthusiastic Rotarians of Rotary club of Dhanbad. They introduced us with the system and the camp. The crowd consisted of old people, women and a large part being children between age of 3 and 10. Most of the children were poor fed and required protein powder. This was given free along with the free medication provided to them. Free food and chocolates were also provided to the children. Their smile made us feel contented to our soul. And then with a heart full of joy and pride, we returned!
Regards
Rotaract Club Of Sindri
